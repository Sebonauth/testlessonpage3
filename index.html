Now, let's explore gradient descent in two dimensions.

Function:

\[ f(x, y) = x^2 + y^2 \]

This function represents a bowl-shaped surface with a minimum at \((0, 0)\).

Starting from \((x_0, y_0) = (3, 4)\) and using \(\alpha = 0.1\).

Let's perform one gradient descent iteration.

Compute the gradients:

\[ \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = 2x \implies \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}(3, 4) = 2 \times 3 = 6 \] \[ \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = 2y \implies \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}(3, 4) = 2 \times 4 = 8 \]

Continue

Update \(x\) and \(y\):

\[ x_1 = x_0 - \alpha \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = 3 - 0.1 \times 6 = 3 - 0.6 = 2.4 \] \[ y_1 = y_0 - \alpha \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = 4 - 0.1 \times 8 = 4 - 0.8 = 3.2 \]

Answer: After one iteration, \((x, y) = (2.4, 3.2)\).
